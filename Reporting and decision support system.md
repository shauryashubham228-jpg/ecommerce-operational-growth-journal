# Inventory Reporting & Decision Support System — Google Sheets & Excel Automation

---

# Project Overview

## Project Name
Inventory Reporting & Marketplace Decision Support Dashboard

## Industry
D2C Ecommerce — Kids Fashion

## Stakeholders
- Listing Team
- Performance Marketing Team
- Inventory Operations Team
- Marketplace Management Team

---

# Project Objective

Build a centralized reporting and decision-support system using Google Sheets and Excel to help multiple teams:
- analyze inventory movement
- identify discounting opportunities
- monitor top-selling products
- identify slow-moving inventory
- detect zero-sale inventory
- support marketplace listing actions
- improve collaboration and operational efficiency

The system was designed to reduce dependency on large master sheets and enable faster decision-making through searchable and filter-based reporting views.

---

# Business Problem

The operations and marketplace teams were facing challenges because:

- the master inventory sheet was large and difficult to navigate
- identifying actionable SKUs required manual searching
- multiple teams needed different inventory views
- performance marketing required product-level filtering for ads
- listing teams needed quick identification of products requiring discounting
- inventory tracking across marketplaces was time-consuming

This created operational inefficiencies in:
- inventory decision-making
- marketplace discount planning
- ad campaign selection
- task allocation
- cross-team collaboration

---

# Solution Approach

A structured reporting and filtering system was built using:
- Google Sheets
- Excel formulas
- Pivot tables
- Dynamic search tables
- Filter-based reporting
- Slicers
- Charts & visual summaries

The system acted as a lightweight operational dashboard for inventory and marketplace actions.

---

# Core Features Implemented

# 1. Master Inventory Sheet

A centralized master sheet was maintained containing:
- SKU details
- inventory quantity
- sales performance
- marketplace status
- discounting requirements
- inventory movement status

## Collaboration Features
Teams could:
- apply filters
- write comments
- highlight/color-code rows
- track required actions collaboratively

---

# 2. Search & Filter Decision Table

## Objective
Reduce dependency on navigating the large master sheet.

A separate searchable filter table was created where teams could:
- enter values
- select filters
- dynamically generate focused inventory views

This enabled faster operational decision-making without manually searching the master sheet.

---

# Use Cases of the Search & Filter Table

## Marketplace Discounting

Listing teams could quickly identify:
- slow-moving inventory
- products needing discounts
- low-conversion SKUs
- overstocked inventory

This helped prioritize:
- marketplace discount campaigns
- pricing actions
- listing optimization

---

## Performance Marketing Campaign Selection

Performance marketing teams used the filter/search table to identify:
- top-selling products
- high-performing SKUs
- products suitable for ad campaigns
- inventory with sufficient stock for scaling ads

This helped:
- optimize ad spend allocation
- avoid advertising low-stock products
- improve campaign targeting

---

## Zero-Sale Inventory Identification

The filter table helped operations teams identify:
- products with zero sales
- inactive SKUs
- dead inventory
- low-performing categories

This supported:
- liquidation planning
- inventory cleanup decisions
- discounting strategies

---

## Task Allocation Across Teams

Instead of sharing the entire master sheet:
- filtered views were generated
- actionable rows were isolated
- teams could focus only on relevant products

This improved:
- operational clarity
- workflow segregation
- execution speed
